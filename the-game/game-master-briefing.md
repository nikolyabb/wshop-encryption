# Game Master Guide

> For the Game Master (GM): Teacher or Student Leader.

## The Setup

- Make 3 Teams: where **Team 1** and **Team 3** might be smaller than **Team 2**
- **Team 1** and **Team 3** are playing against **Team 2**
- The goal of The Game is successfull collaboration **Team 1** and **Team 3** who share knowledge of the Secret Algorithm
- **Team 2** is on the critical path in between **Team 1** and **Team 3**, because only this team can deliver the Message from **Team 1** to **Team 3**
- Every Team should promote The Delegate - the one who can interact with the next Team. All the other team members can not get closer to each other close than 1 meter without a Warning
- **Team 1** and **Team 3** should have no way of direct communication with each other
- Within the rules above, **Team 2** could do any eavesdropping or attempts to overlook work of the other Teams without breaking safety perimeter of 1 meter
- GM decides how much warnings has to be issued before stop the game and anounce the winner if safety perimeter of 1 meter is being broken
- Do not provide a lot of operational guidance at first to the **Team 1** and **Team 3**, to let the "secret is compromised" scenario happen if they would be too loud
- GM can extend or reduce timer to maintain the dynamics of the game. Optionally, music could be used
- GM can write the **Alphabet** (A‑Z) on the board for reference or make it visible that **Team 1** and **Team 3** works with **Alphabet board** 
- GM informs everyone that claiming success should be done by shouting "MESSAGE SECURED!"

## Scenario 1: The “Plus One” Cipher (Weak Encryption). 

### Nubmer of Rounds. 
There might be 2-4 or more rounds played until it is crystal clear for **Team 2** how to break the code.

### Shared Rule  
Change every letter to the next letter in the alphabet  
`A → B, B → C, …, Z → A`.

| Team | Instruction |
|-------|-------------|
| Team 1 | “Shift every letter forward by one.” |
| Team 3 | “Shift every letter backward by one.” |
| Team 2 | **No hints in 1st round, small hint in 2nd round, big hint in 3rd round** |

If somehow this communication of GM and **Team 1** is being exposed to the **Team 2**, the direction or length of the shift could be changed to save the Round.  

### Flow

1. **Team 1** has 120 seconds to pick a word (e.g., `CAT`) and encrypt it with provided knowledge of The Rule. As result it writes (e.g. `DBU`) on the paper and pass it to **Team 2**. If they fail to do this - everyone loses with **Ending #1**, because no message was delivered and nothing intercepted.
2. When **Team 2** receives the message another 180 seconds timer starts. That is time to break the code. If they are able to do it - it is a win for **Team 2** with **Ending #2**. After 180 seconds message must be delivered to **Team 3**.
3. **Team 3** might hurry up **Team 2** to add pressure and sense of urgency, which should add more fun to the game, but that is up to GM to decide if that's acceptable. It balances the game: **Team 2** has time but no key. **Team 3** has the key but no time.
4. For **Team 2** there is implicit but acceptable move - to make a copy of the message, which enable them to continue breaking the code along with **Team 3**. If they succeed before **Team 3** that counts a win with **Ending #2**.
5. **Team 3** recieves the Message and another 180 seconds timer starts. Extra time helps them to get together after long delay and optionally contributes to success of **Team 2**.
If **Team 3** use their Secret Algorithm properly before **Team 2** hidden activity suceeds, thats a win for **Team 3** and **Team 1** with **Ending #3**.

### Hints

**GM's Small Hint for Team 2:**  
*“The Spies are lazy. They didn't want to do difficult math, so they barely changed the letters at all. Look at the alphabet board.”*. 

**GM's Medium Hint for Team 2:**  
*"Look at the first letter. If the Spies wrote 'B', they actually meant 'A'. If they wrote 'D', they meant 'C'. They are just taking one step forward."*

**GM's Big Hint for Team 2:**  
*“To break this code, just take every letter and move one step BACK in the alphabet. 'B' becomes 'A'.”*

### Lessons to learn  
* If the pattern is too simple, the enemy can guess it easily!
* Security is a shared responsibility
* The more people involved the weaker security is

## Scenario 2: The Pigpen Cipher (Strong Encryption). 
This scenario doesn't give a lot of chances to **Team 2**, it would be good to make sure they got a round or two in the provious Scenario to make it feel fair.  

We might want to turn the game for **Team 2** making part of the team Secret Agents and provide them with instructions on how to spy. Or allow few more extra Warnings. It will be secribed at the end of the document. TBD.

Optionally, parents cound be invited to the game as **Team 2** while students spread over **Team 1** and **Team 3**.

### Shared Rule  
Change every letter into a shape based on the Grid Key.

| Team | Instruction |
|-------|-------------|
| Team 1 & 3 | Receive the **Pigpen Cheat Sheet** (Artifact 4). |
| Team 2 | **Big hint in 1st round** |

### Flow

1. **Team 1** picks a word (e.g., `MAP`) and draws the shapes. 120 seconds.
2. They pass the paper to **Team 2**. Start the timer - 180 seconds. Shorter timer will help to improve the game dynamics. If necessary, GM could extend timer.
3. **Team 2** will likely fail.
4. Pass the paper to **Team 3**. They match shapes to their key and solve it instantly.

### Hints
**GM big hint for Team 2:**  
   *“It looks like Tic‑Tac‑Toe… but without the key, can you solve it?”*

### Lessons to learn  
* When you have a Secure Shared Key, the message is safe even if the enemy sees it!



## Addendum to Scenario 2: Secret Spy - Social Engineering.  
Since space is tight, **Team 2** can use that to their advantage. Proximity makes "Spying" easier!

#### Explaining Tic-Tac-Toe (Without spoiling the cipher)

The Trick: Do not explain it during the game. Explain it as a "warm-up" activity before you even mention spies.

GM Action: "Okay class, before we start, who knows how to play Tic-Tac-Toe? Let's draw a grid on the board." Play one quick round on the board. Then erase the X's and O's but leave the empty grid lines on the board.

Later, when Team 2 sees the Pigpen cipher, that empty grid on the board becomes a massive visual subliminal hint.

#### Social Engineering Mechanics (How Team 2 can cheat) Since Team 2 will lose the Pigpen round instantly if they play fair, give them these "Cheat Cards" or abilities:

* The "Drone" (The Scout):

Allows one member of Team 2 to stand up and walk one full circle around Team 1 (staying 1 meter away).

Goal: Peek at the cipher sheet Team 1 is holding.

* "Trash Diving":

If Team 1 messes up a drawing and crumples the paper to throw it away (or puts it on the desk), that paper is fair game. Team 2 can take it.

GM Note: This teaches Team 1 that they must destroy their sensitive trash!

* The "Mole" (The Impostor) - Risky but fun:

Pick one student in Team 1 to be a "Mole."

They cannot speak to Team 2, but if they scratch their head, it means "Top Grid." If they scratch their nose, it means "Dot Grid." (This might be too complex for elementary, but "Trash Diving" usually works wonders).
